Conway's Game of Life for Vices

Experimentation Game Algorithmic Practice 2

This is an experiment to implement a simple game that embraces a spiritual concept.

“The reason why the Saints were so perfectly recollected is that they always sought to abstain from wordly desires, leaving themselves free to give their whole heart to God.“
— Imitation of Christ Book 1, Chp. 12

This focuses on what it looks like to remove vices as quickly as possible (within the rules of the game)

Similar to Conway's Game of Life, you select certain tiles on cells to make them vices, then you simply observe the changes between Grace and vice.

0.1 Vice

What is it?
I've defined it as literally anything you're attatched to. Everything will be gone from you at the end of your life, so overeagerness for anything: to keep your friends, your health, to do well in school, works against you.1—3

Implementation
The table is empty at first. Clicking and dragging over cells make them vices. If a cell is a vice it can either:

  • Harden— being surrounded by vices make them harder to remove
  • Go Away— if they're not surrounded you can remove them youself, otherwise you'll need the help of Grace.
  • Grow—vices can spread to other empty cells

0.2 Grace

What is it?
I've defined as literally anything you're attatched to. It is in our best effort to leave ourselves free for God. Everything will gone from you at the end of your life, so overeagerness anything: to keep your friends, your health, to do well in school, is working against you.1—3

Implementation
The table is empty at first. Clicking and dragging over cells make them vices. If a cell is a vice it can either:

  • Harden— being surrounded by vices make them harder to remove
  • Go Away— if they're not surrounded you can remove them youself, otherwise you'll need the help of Grace.
  • Grow—vices can spread to other empty cells

Reference

"All things are vanity" (Eccl 1:2)

3. To reject all things is to have mastery over them all.
